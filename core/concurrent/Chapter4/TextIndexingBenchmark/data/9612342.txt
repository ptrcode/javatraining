 1967 Cinema West German film, starring Anita Pallenberg and directed by Volker Schlöndorff.  The film is widely recognised because of the soundtrack composed, produced, arranged, and played by Brian Jones (founder of the Rolling Stones), Pallenbergs boyfriend at the time. The film was entered into the 1967 Cannes Film Festival.   

It was filmed in colour in West Germany in 1967.

==Plot==
Marie (Anita Pallenberg) shoots her ex-boyfriend with his own gun, after he attempts to beat her.  Instead of reporting this to the police she hires two men to help her dump the body in a construction site near an autobahn.  While doing this she becomes romantically involved with both men.

Rumors also persist that Keith Richards played bass guitar on the soundtrack.
